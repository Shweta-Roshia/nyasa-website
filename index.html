About Nyasa

Nyasa (meaning: trust) began in 2011 to provide a safe, learning‑rich environment for children of migrant construction workers and the neighbouring communities. We combine practical support (meals, clothes, school supplies) with learning opportunities and community health outreach.

Mission

Provide consistent education, health awareness and skills so that children and families have access to better opportunities and dignity.

Approach

Holistic — we blend classroom learning, extracurricular exposure, regular health camps and targeted skill training for adults to create long‑term change.

Programs & Activities

Open‑air school & Summer Camp

Arts, origami, games, basic literacy and numeracy camps that keep children engaged and learning during holidays.

Sanjeevani Medical Camps

Periodic outreach health camps offering free checkups, medicines, awareness sessions for women and children.

Umeed — Skill Development

Computer basics and English classes for contractual workers to improve employability.

Impact & Stories

Numbers

Reaching 4000+ people each year through combined programs and camps. Hundreds of children enrolled over the past decade.

Pathway to school

We help children transition from our informal programs into formal schools, including support for uniforms, books and follow up.

Volunteer stories

Students and faculty share experiences: from teaching a class to organising a medical camp — Nyasa is built by volunteers.

Support Nyasa

Donate or volunteer — every contribution helps.
